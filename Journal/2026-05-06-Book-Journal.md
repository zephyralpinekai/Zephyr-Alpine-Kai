# My First Reading Journal: 2026 Pulitzer Thoughts

This is my first blog posted on GitHub. I will be writing about my recent reading responses and thoughts while reading the 2026 Pulitzer Prize winner books.

Lightweight concrete uses special aggregates instead of regular stone to cut weight and improve insulation. For structural use, the most common are expanded shale, clay, slate, pumice, scoria, expanded slag, and sintered fly ash. For non-structural insulation, perlite and vermiculite work well. Perlite is expanded volcanic glass that forms lightweight white beads. Vermiculite is a mica-based mineral that expands like an accordion into soft, golden-brown flakes, trapping lots of air for great insulation.
